# Public Evidence-Risk Utility Surface v1

## Utility Surface Statement

Public Evidence-Risk Utility Surface v1 makes Hoax.ai visibly useful. Visitors can read manual checklists, follow an evidence posture map, study synthetic examples, and work through guided evidence-risk questions — without upload, scoring, verdicts, fake/real labels, public API, automated reports, or detector positioning.

## Why Public Utility Is Necessary

After Sprint 83 corrected public copy boundaries, Hoax.ai still risked reading as governance-only infrastructure. Production contact matters: people must encounter practical reference utilities on the public surface, not only thesis prose and internal validators.

## What Was Built

| Route | Utility |
|-------|---------|
| `/manual-evidence-checklist/` | Seven-group manual checklist |
| `/evidence-posture-map/` | HTML/CSS visual posture map and reading cards |
| `/synthetic-examples/` | Five synthetic evidence-risk scenarios |
| `/evidence-risk-questions/` | Guided public question flows |

Homepage links all four utilities under **Use Hoax.ai as an Evidence-Risk Utility**.

## How Visitors Benefit

- Immediate manual tools for editorial, institutional, and personal evidence review
- Shared vocabulary for posture before conclusion
- Safe synthetic literacy without real-world case risk
- Clear boundaries before sharing, rejecting, accusing, or escalating

## Why This Is Not a Detector

These utilities do not accept uploads, run automated analysis, emit scores, issue fake/real labels, certify truth, or generate investigation reports. They guide thinking — they do not judge artifacts for users.

## Boundary Rules

- Static HTML/CSS only
- No JavaScript, forms, APIs, or tracking
- No real-world cases involving identifiable parties
- No detector, upload, score, verdict, or automated-report language as product claims
- Artifact-first, non-accusatory posture language

## Utility Value

Hoax.ai is useful without becoming a detector. Public utility first. Governance second. Validators protect what was built.
