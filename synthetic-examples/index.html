Synthetic Evidence-Risk Examples

These five synthetic scenarios illustrate how evidence-risk reading works. They use fictional situations only — no real people, companies, political events, legal disputes, or current events. They do not issue verdicts, scores, or fake/real labels.

Example 1 — Source Gap, Stable Context

Synthetic scenario
A cropped image circulates with a detailed caption describing a public ceremony. The ceremony type is plausible and contextually stable, but the original publisher is unnamed.
Evidence condition
Context reads coherent; source identity is missing.
Risk signal
Readers may treat contextual plausibility as source verification.
Boundary warning
Stable context does not repair an unknown source path.
Safe posture language
Source unidentified; contextual fit partial; provenance unsettled.
What not to conclude
Do not treat the image as confirmed because the caption “sounds right.”
Better question to ask
Who first published this artifact and where can that path be inspected?

Example 2 — Provenance Weak, Claim Overextended

Synthetic scenario
A short audio clip is shared with a claim that it records a specific policy announcement. Metadata is stripped; only a repost account is visible.
Evidence condition
Provenance chain broken; public claim exceeds artifact support.
Risk signal
Claim drift from “audio exists” to “announcement happened as described.”
Boundary warning
Without custody and origin, the clip cannot carry announcement-level weight.
Safe posture language
Provenance gap; claim exceeds artifact envelope; posture limited.
What not to conclude
Do not treat repost virality as provenance.
Better question to ask
What portion of the claim is audible fact versus interpretive addition?

Example 3 — Traceability Broken, No Verdict Possible

Synthetic scenario
A screenshot of a document is forwarded through three anonymous channels. No link to an original file, issuer, or timestamp chain remains.
Evidence condition
Traceability absent; artifact isolated from source record.
Risk signal
Pressure to decide quickly despite missing chain.
Boundary warning
Without traceability, posture vocabulary should stay at limitation — not accusation.
Safe posture language
Not assessable beyond screenshot form; traceability absent.
What not to conclude
Do not infer issuer intent or institutional approval from the image alone.
Better question to ask
What minimum chain would be required before this artifact could support any action?

Example 4 — Context Collapse, High Interpretation Risk

Synthetic scenario
A ten-second video fragment is edited out of a longer recording. The fragment shows a gesture without surrounding dialogue or location markers.
Evidence condition
Context collapsed; interpretive room expanded.
Risk signal
High interpretation risk; appearance drives narrative.
Boundary warning
Stripped context increases overreading danger — not misconduct proof.
Safe posture language
Context collapse; interpretation risk elevated; artifact incomplete.
What not to conclude
Do not assign motive or character judgment from the fragment.
Better question to ask
What surrounding context would be required to read this gesture proportionately?

Example 5 — Claim Drift from Artifact to Accusation

Synthetic scenario
A generic stock-style portrait is paired with text implying institutional misconduct. The image itself shows only a neutral headshot with no institutional markers.
Evidence condition
Artifact subject matter minimal; accusatory framing added externally.
Risk signal
Claim drift from image presence to subject guilt narrative.
Boundary warning
Artifact–subject separation violated in circulation, not in posture vocabulary.
Safe posture language
Claim drift detected; artifact does not support accusatory scope.
What not to conclude
Do not transfer narrative accusation onto a neutral image artifact.
Better question to ask
What evidence object actually supports the misconduct claim — if any?

Non-Verdict Boundary

These examples teach evidence-risk literacy. They are not case files, investigations, or detection results. Hoax.ai does not evaluate real uploads or issue automated judgments.
