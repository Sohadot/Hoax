Entry purpose

Trust and safety teams need shared language for ambiguous evidence conditions — especially when policy, escalation, and external communication must stay bounded. Hoax.ai offers definitional vocabulary; it does not operate moderation queues, issue strikes, or determine enforcement outcomes.

Platform reviewers frequently encounter claim drift, stripped context, unclear sources, and broken traceability. Hoax.ai concepts name those conditions so policy writers and frontline reviewers can align on what is known before decisions.

Boundary Integrity and Not-Assessable Posture help teams refuse overclaim in public statements and user communications. Pathways route reviewers through concept sequences when a case pattern matches a known evidence condition.

This entry point is for vocabulary and training — not for substituting Hoax.ai for internal investigation tools, legal review, or vendor detection products.

Policy language fails when teams imply certainty the evidence chain cannot support. Claim Drift and Context Collapse give reviewers phrases that describe circulation damage without accusing users of intent.

Source Ambiguity and Traceability Gap help escalation briefings separate what is unknown about origin from what is unknown about content — a distinction enforcement narratives often collapse.

The Claim Overextended pathway and Context Missing pathway train reviewers to recognize common case shapes and route to the right concept definitions during onboarding.

Boundary Integrity should appear in style guides for public statements: Hoax.ai vocabulary must not be quoted as if the platform certified truth or performed detection.

When internal tools cannot resolve a case, Not-Assessable Posture supports honest user communication — describing limits instead of fabricating confidence.

Link new hires to this entry point alongside Why Hoax.ai is not a detector and return to Hoax.ai Entry Points when teams need education or research-oriented paths for cross-functional partners.

Start here

Begin with Claim Drift and Context Collapse — the most common circulation conditions in platform integrity work — then read Boundary Integrity before drafting external language.

Entry-point pages teach how to enter Hoax.ai by role without producing a conclusion. They route visitors toward stable vocabulary, manual utilities, governed reference definitions, and condition-based pathways. Return to Hoax.ai Entry Points or the homepage whenever you need to reorient.

Follow this path through Hoax.ai routes matched to this entry point:

  1. Step 1: Claim Drift. Assertions that change as material circulates.
  2. Step 2: Context Collapse. Framing stripped from original setting.
  3. Step 3: Source Ambiguity. Unclear publisher or authority reading.
  4. Step 4: Traceability Gap. Broken links between artifact and claim.
  5. Step 5: Boundary Integrity. Preserve category limits in policy language.
  6. Step 6: Not-Assessable Posture. When posture must stay limited.
  7. Step 7: Claim Overextended pathway. When assertions outrun artifacts.
  8. Step 8: Context Missing pathway. When framing was removed.
  9. Step 9: Why Not a Detector. Boundary before tool assumptions.

What this entry point helps with

  • Shared vocabulary for policy writers and frontline reviewers
  • Training materials that avoid detector or verdict framing
  • External communication that names evidence limits clearly
  • Escalation briefings that separate conditions from outcomes
  • Cross-team alignment on not-assessable posture cases

What this entry point cannot do

  • Moderation decisions, strikes, or enforcement automation
  • Replacing internal investigation or legal escalation paths
  • Binary authenticity labels for user-facing case resolution
  • Numeric certainty outputs for policy thresholds
  • Vendor detection substitution or scoring infrastructure
