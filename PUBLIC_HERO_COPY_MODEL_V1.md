# Public Hero Copy Model v1

## Approved Hero Headline

**Truth is no longer the first layer.  
Evidence is.**

## Approved Subheadline

Hoax.ai helps people read evidence risk in the synthetic media age — without issuing verdicts, scores, uploads, or fake/real labels.

## Approved Supporting Paragraph

Hoax.ai is a governed public evidence-risk reference system. It guides people through manual evidence posture thinking — source, provenance, context, traceability, claim drift, and boundary integrity — without judging artifacts for users or issuing automated verdicts.

## Rejected Alternatives

| Rejected headline | Why rejected |
|-------------------|--------------|
| Hoax.ai is not a truth machine. | Defensive negation; defines category by denial rather than utility |
| Detect synthetic media with Hoax.ai | Implies detector product |
| Verify evidence authenticity | Implies truth certification |
| Upload and analyze your media | Implies upload tool |
| AI-powered deepfake detector | Implies classifier/score engine |

## Rationale for Hero Change

“Hoax.ai is not a truth machine” correctly states a boundary but poorly defines the category. Visitors encounter a negation before understanding what Hoax.ai offers. The evidence-first headline states the category shift (evidence before truth-as-default) while subheadline copy preserves strict non-verdict, non-upload, non-score boundaries.

## Boundary Statement Retention

The sentence “Hoax.ai is not a truth machine” may remain as a boundary statement in “What Hoax.ai is not” sections. It must not serve as the primary H1 or category-defining headline.

## Homepage Section Model

1. Hero — evidence-first headline and utility subheadline
2. Guided questions — manual evidence-risk inquiry
3. What Hoax.ai is — utility and reference framing
4. What Hoax.ai is not — detector/upload/score/verdict denials
5. Evidence posture map preview — conceptual non-verdict map
6. Next public utility direction — future manual utilities (not created in Sprint 83 unless authorized)
