## External Review Readiness Statement

Hoax.ai adopts five public external-review readiness routes that help human reviewers and AI agents inspect the public reference system through structured public inspection paths. External review means walking utilities, concepts, pathways, strategic layers, and boundary pages — not transaction solicitation, case evaluation, or operational tool use.

## Why external review readiness was needed after Sprint 100

Sprint 100 validated release integrity across 58 routes and added a homepage Public Release Integrity Snapshot. Reviewers still needed explicit guidance for how to inspect the public surface, AI retrieval behavior, and boundary discipline without confusing Hoax.ai for a detector, transaction page, or legal or financial representation.

## Human usability role

External-review pages give reviewers a front door, area map, static checklist, AI guide, and boundary guide without forms, scoring, or interactive tooling.

## AI retrieval role

Reference Answer, Source Confidence, Cite This Reference, and Retrieval Capsule blocks on each route support agent-safe quoting and boundary-aware summarization.

## Non-verdict boundary

Hoax.ai helps people read evidence conditions before conclusions. It does not produce automated authenticity labels, numeric certainty outputs, verdicts, uploads, or public reports.

## Non-transactional boundary

External-review pages support public review of the Hoax.ai reference surface. They are not transaction pages, pricing statements, acquisition term documents, representative mandates, legal representations, or financial representations.
