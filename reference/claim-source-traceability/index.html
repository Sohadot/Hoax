Claim–Source Traceability

Claim–Source Traceability must exist because reference integrity collapses when statements outrun their source basis—a core discipline for sovereign reference layers.

Definition

Claim–Source Traceability is the Hoax.ai term for the governed relationship between a claim and the source material used to support, limit, qualify, or reject that claim within repository and public reference discipline.

It is structural integrity vocabulary: claims must remain traceable to governed sources and support locations. Traceability does not prove a claim true and does not certify a source.

Category Thesis

Hoax.ai cannot be a sovereign reference layer if public statements float free of source discipline. Claim–Source Traceability makes traceability feel necessary—not optional metadata.

Why This Term Is Necessary

SEO pages, detector marketing, and informal fact-check culture all reward confident statements. Traceability instead asks what source basis exists before a claim carries public weight.

Why It Matters

Researchers, analysts, journalists, legal reviewers, and platform integrity teams need traceability language that prevents unsupported certainty without turning mapping into truth certification.

What It Can Describe

Claim–Source Traceability can describe mapping between ledger claims, source registry entries, support locations, public surface references, and boundary rules that keep claims proportionate to source strength.

What It Cannot Decide

Claim–Source Traceability does not prove a claim true. It does not certify a source. It does not replace evidence review, forensic analysis, or institutional adjudication.

What This Concept Prevents

It prevents unsupported certainty: statements that sound authoritative without source basis. It prevents citation theater and prevents reference pages from inventing external factual weight.

System Role

Claim–Source Traceability connects Source Confidence, Evidence Chain, Output Boundary, and Evidence Posture. Traceability strengthens chain discipline; output boundary limits how traced claims may be phrased publicly.

Practical Reading Frame

Imagine a fictional editor reviews a draft reference sentence asserting a governance milestone. The sentence maps to an audit file, a decision log entry, and a validator path. No external entity is named.

Claim–Source Traceability language confirms the statement's weight is proportionate to repository sources. That does not prove the claim true in an absolute sense—it proves structural discipline for public reference integrity.

Common Misreadings

  • Misreading: Traceability means fact-check approval. Correction: It is repository mapping discipline—not external adjudication.
  • Misreading: Mapped claims are automatically true. Correction: Mapping prevents overstatement; it does not certify truth.
