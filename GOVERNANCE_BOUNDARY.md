# Hoax.ai Governance Boundary

## Purpose

This document defines what Hoax.ai may and may not claim, classify, publish, or imply.

Hoax.ai operates in a high-risk category: synthetic media, deception, impersonation, misinformation, forged evidence, and digital trust.

For that reason, the project must be governed from the first public version.

## Core Boundary

Hoax.ai is not a truth machine.

It does not declare reality.

It does not issue final verdicts about individuals, institutions, events, media, or claims.

It classifies the condition of evidence.

## What Hoax.ai May Do

Hoax.ai may classify:

- evidence posture;
- source confidence;
- synthetic risk signals;
- provenance weaknesses;
- contextual gaps;
- media coherence signals;
- identity uncertainty;
- deception-pattern alignment;
- verification limitations;
- and recommended next checks.

Hoax.ai may explain why a piece of content, claim, or media requires further examination.

Hoax.ai may provide structured outputs that help trust-and-safety, brand protection, digital risk, platform integrity, and security teams understand evidence risk.

## What Hoax.ai Must Not Do

Hoax.ai must not:

- label people as liars;
- accuse individuals or institutions of deception;
- claim that a person, company, government, or organization committed fraud;
- present itself as a legal authority;
- present itself as a political fact-checking authority;
- claim to detect all deepfakes;
- claim to prove authenticity;
- claim to certify truth;
- convert uncertainty into accusation;
- publish sensational or reputationally harmful claims without evidence governance;
- or imply certainty where the evidence does not support certainty.

## Required Language Discipline

Preferred language:

- evidence posture;
- source confidence;
- synthetic risk signal;
- provenance gap;
- contextual uncertainty;
- verification boundary;
- bounded assessment;
- deception-pattern indicator.

Avoid or restrict:

- fake;
- real;
- proven;
- certified truth;
- guaranteed detection;
- verified hoax;
- confirmed deception;
- fraud;
- liar;
- definitive proof.

## Output Principle

Every output must communicate bounded confidence.

No output should collapse complexity into a careless binary.

Approved framing:

> This assessment identifies evidence-risk signals and verification gaps. It does not issue a final truth verdict.

## Governance Rule

**Hoax.ai classifies evidence posture only. It does not perform the verdict.**

## Artifact–Subject Separation Doctrine

Hoax.ai classifies evidence artifacts. It does not classify the people, institutions, brands, organizations, or events connected to those artifacts.

An artifact is a discrete item of digital evidence: an image, video, audio recording, document, screenshot, source record, claim record, or media object.

A high-risk evidence posture on an artifact is a statement about the artifact's evidentiary condition: its provenance, coherence, verifiability, source confidence, contextual stability, and risk signals.

It is not, and must never be presented as:

- a statement that a connected person is deceptive, guilty, dishonest, or involved;
- a statement that a connected institution acted in bad faith;
- a statement that a connected event did or did not occur;
- a legal, factual, moral, or reputational judgment about the subject.

A weak, unstable, or high-risk evidence artifact connected to a person does not transfer that weakness to the person.

Evidence about evidence is not evidence about people.

Where an artifact depicts or references an identifiable subject, Hoax.ai confines its classification to the artifact's evidentiary condition and explicitly declines to characterize the subject.
