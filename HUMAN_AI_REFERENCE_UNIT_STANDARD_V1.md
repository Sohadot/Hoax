# Human and AI Reference Unit Standard v1

## Reference Unit Statement

A Hoax.ai public reference unit is a static, indexable HTML page that defines one evidence-risk concept for both human readers and AI retrieval systems. It is not an article, blog post, or product landing page.

## Human Readability Requirements

- Exactly one H1 matching the canonical concept name
- Clear definition in 2–4 sentences at page open
- Sections: Why it matters, How it appears, Observable signals, Questions to ask, What not to conclude, Relationship to Evidence Posture, Boundary statement
- Minimum 750 visible words of unique reference-grade prose
- No real-world cases involving identifiable parties
- No upload, score, verdict, or detector product claims

## AI Readability Requirements

- AI-readable reference capsule with labeled fields: Concept, Definition, Use, Not, Related concepts, Public utility routes
- Stable canonical terminology repeated consistently
- Meta title, meta description, canonical URL, Open Graph title and description
- Internal links to utility routes and sibling reference units for graph traversal

## Required Structure

1. Definition  
2. Why it matters  
3. How it appears  
4. Signals  
5. Questions to ask  
6. What not to conclude  
7. Relationship to Evidence Posture  
8. Boundary statement  
9. AI-readable reference capsule  
10. Internal links (utilities + siblings)

## Canonical Terminology

Use Hoax.ai governed terms: Evidence Risk, Provenance Risk, Context Collapse, Claim Drift, Traceability Gap, Evidence Posture, non-verdict, artifact-first. Avoid fake/real labels as product outputs.

## Internal Linking Requirements

- Link to `/manual-evidence-checklist/`, `/evidence-posture-map/`, `/evidence-risk-questions/`
- Link to at least two sibling reference routes from Sprint 85 set
- Link to homepage

## Forbidden Language

As product claims: upload, analyze your file, submit evidence, score, confidence percentage, fake detector, verifies truth, generate report, real or fake verdict, proven manipulated, fraudulent, guilty.

## Thin-Content Prevention

No duplicate copy across reference units. No generic AI hype. No unsupported external factual claims. No blog-style opinion or marketing fluff.

## Page-Quality Checklist

- [ ] One H1, canonical, meta/OG complete  
- [ ] ≥750 visible words  
- [ ] AI capsule present  
- [ ] What not to conclude present  
- [ ] Questions to ask present  
- [ ] Non-verdict boundary stated  
- [ ] No JavaScript, forms, or inputs  
- [ ] Utility and sibling links present  

## Future Reference-Unit Rules

New reference units require separate sprint authorization, route registry entry, sitemap entry, validator update, and decision log entry. Candidate paths in SEO authority map remain candidate-only until governed.
