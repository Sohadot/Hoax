# Internal Prototype Traceability Failure Modes

## Traceability Failure Statement

Traceability fails whenever an internal prototype result cannot be structurally linked back to governed source authority, protocol logic, boundary checks, caveat triggers, or guardrail protections.

## Orphan Posture Failure

A posture candidate exists without protocol-step, standard-principle, or evidence-condition support.

**Required response:** fail validation and repair posture basis references.

## Orphan Caveat Failure

A caveat trigger appears without corresponding boundary or condition source.

**Required response:** fail validation and restore boundary-to-caveat linkage.

## Orphan Boundary Failure

A boundary check appears without traceable source condition or protocol anchor.

**Required response:** fail validation and attach evidence-condition source references.

## Missing Guardrail Failure

A prohibited family or transformation block is referenced without guardrail rule linkage.

**Required response:** fail validation and restore guardrail-to-transformation mapping.

## Forbidden Transformation Invisibility

The system blocks an unsafe output family but cannot explain which forbidden transformation rule was responsible.

**Required response:** fail validation and add forbidden transformation refs.

## Not-Assessable Opacity

A `Not Assessable` result exists without limitation reasons.

**Required response:** fail validation and attach limitation reason refs.

## Out-of-Scope Opacity

An `Out of Scope` result exists without scope reasons.

**Required response:** fail validation and attach out-of-scope reason refs.

## Report-Shape Drift

Structured traceability turns into report-shaped or narrative output.

**Required response:** fail validation and remove narrative rendering behavior.

## Public Explanation Drift

Internal interpretability starts to resemble public transparency UI or rendered explanation output.

**Required response:** fail validation and restore internal-only structured form.

## Debug-to-Product Drift

Traceability tooling begins acting like a public engine, dashboard, API, or product feature.

**Required response:** fail validation and remove public-facing behavior.
