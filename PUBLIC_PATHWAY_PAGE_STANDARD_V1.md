# Public Pathway Page Standard v1

## Pathway Page Standard Statement

Public pathway pages guide visitors through Hoax.ai evidence-risk concepts by condition. They are static, indexable, citation-safe, and non-verdict.

## Required Page Structure

1. Reference summary — one sentence pathway overview
2. Pathway purpose — condition the pathway addresses
3. Start here — first concept to inspect and why
4. Pathway Steps — five to seven steps with internal links
5. Questions to ask — practical non-verdict questions
6. What not to conclude — forbidden leaps
7. Relationship to Evidence Posture — posture impact without verdict
8. Reference Answer — question, short answer, use/do-not-use, related pages
9. Source Confidence — support type, can/cannot support, boundary
10. Reference Path — navigation breadcrumb
11. Cite This Reference — citation metadata
12. Retrieval Capsule — AI retrieval unit
13. Related Concepts — contextual links
14. Page-end reference navigation — utilities, concepts, pathways
15. Boundary reminder — non-verdict discipline

## Minimum Content Depth

At least 850 visible words of unique pathway-grade content per route.

## Human Readability Requirements

One H1, clear section headings, practical questions, and condition-first framing.

## AI Readability Requirements

Stable anchor IDs, Retrieval Capsule, Reference Answer, and canonical URLs.

## Citation/Retrieval Requirements

Cite This Reference and Retrieval Capsule blocks on every pathway page.

## Source-Confidence Requirements

Source Confidence block with support type, boundaries, and related reference links.

## Internal Link Requirements

At least 3 utility routes, 5 reference routes, and 2 sibling pathway routes per page.

## Boundary Requirements

Explicit non-verdict boundary reminder; no verdict, score, upload, or detector language.

## Forbidden Patterns

Forbidden exact n-grams include real or fake, fake detector, verifies truth, scores authenticity, upload a file, generate report, verified true, proven manipulated, fraudulent, guilty, deceptive, and related patterns listed in Sprint 93 governance.

## Thin-Page Prevention Rules

Pathway pages must meet minimum word count and include all required components — not keyword stubs or link dumps.
