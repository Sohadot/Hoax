Definition

Not-assessable posture is the condition where available evidence does not support a responsible evidence posture description due to missing source, provenance, context, traceability, or claim-boundary information. It is a disciplined stop — not a moral label.

Not-assessable does not mean an artifact is false. It does not mean reviewers should treat material as suspicious by default. It means Hoax.ai cannot responsibly describe posture beyond naming what is missing.

The route complements Not Assessable in the governed reference layer and connects to Evidence Weight and Source Ambiguity as practical depth concepts.

Why It Matters

Institutions lose trust when they overclaim from thin chains — and also when they hide behind permanent agnosticism without explaining what is missing. Not-assessable posture names the responsible middle: stop posture description until chain gaps are addressed.

Synthetic media makes incomplete chains common. A disciplined not-assessable reading prevents binary-authenticity pressure while keeping inquiry open.

AI agents need a stable retrieval term for refusal-to-overclaim that is not a detector result and not a score.

Hoax.ai publishes Not-Assessable Posture as a deep reference unit for humans and AI agents. The page is static, citation-safe, and governed — without file submission, without numeric certainty outputs, and without binary authenticity labels.

Readers should use not-assessable posture vocabulary to slow conclusions, improve questions, and connect manual utilities such as the Manual Evidence Checklist, Evidence Posture Map, and Evidence-Risk Questions before acting on incomplete chains.

How It Appears

Not-assessable posture appears when source identity cannot be read, provenance is gapped, context is collapsed, traceability is broken, or claims exceed artifact support — and no additional chain material is available at review time.

It appears when teams face urgent decisions but lack inspectable paths from public claim back to artifact boundaries.

Posture may become assessable later if chain material arrives. Not-assessable describes the present reading, not a permanent verdict on events.

Observable Signals

  • Critical chain segments are missing with no responsible workaround
  • Source ambiguity cannot be reduced at review time
  • Artifact-claim gap too wide for posture language
  • Traceability path incomplete from claim to artifact
  • Boundary integrity cannot be stated from available material
  • Evidence weight too unstable for limited-support description

What Not to Conclude

  • That not-assessable means false, forged, or manipulated
  • That not-assessable means suspicious or wrongdoing by default
  • That not-assessable is a permanent judgment on subjects or events
  • That not-assessable replaces investigation where legally required
  • That Hoax.ai assigns not-assessable labels from uploads or automation
